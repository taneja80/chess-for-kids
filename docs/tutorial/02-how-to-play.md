# ♟ Page 2 — How to Play

Chess is a battle between **two armies** — White and Black — on an **8×8 board**. Your mission: **trap the enemy King** so it has no legal escape. That's called **Checkmate**.

---

## 👑 Meet Your Army

Each side has 16 pieces. Click any piece in the game and Merlin will glow the squares it can reach.

| Piece | Symbol | How it moves | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **King** | ♔ | One square in any direction | Must always be protected — losing it = game over |
| **Queen** | ♕ | Any direction, any distance | The strongest piece — use her wisely |
| **Rook** | ♖ | Straight lines (up/down/left/right) | Great for open files and back‑rank traps |
| **Bishop** | ♗ | Diagonals only | One stays on light squares, the other on dark |
| **Knight** | ♘ | "L" shape (2+1) | The ONLY piece that can **jump over** others |
| **Pawn** | ♙ | One square forward (two on its first move) | Captures **diagonally**. Reaches the end? It **promotes** to any piece! |

### 🎁 Special Moves
- **Castling** — King and Rook swap‑slide to safety in one move (when neither has moved and the path is clear)
- **En passant** — a pawn can capture an opposing pawn that just leapt past it
- **Promotion** — push a pawn to the far rank and a popup lets you choose Queen, Rook, Bishop, or Knight

The app handles all the rules for you — illegal moves simply won't happen. You just click and play.

---

## 🎯 The Goal: Checkmate

- **Check** — the enemy King is under attack. The opponent *must* respond (block, capture, or run).
- **Checkmate** — the King is in check **and** has no legal escape. Game over, you win! 🏆
- **Stalemate** — the player to move has **no legal moves** but is **not in check**. The game is a **draw**.
- **Draw** — also happens by repetition, 50‑move rule, or insufficient material. The app detects all of these.

## 🔮 Merlin's Vision Powers

This is what makes **Chess for Kids** special. Toggle these from the sidebar to "see" the battlefield like a wizard:

### 🟢🔴🟡 Territory Map
Shades the entire board to show **who controls each square**:
- **Green** — your pieces command this square (safe to land on)
- **Red** — the enemy controls it (dangerous!)
- **Yellow** — contested by both armies equally

Use this before every move to spot safe landing zones and avoid walking into traps.

### 🛡️ Golden Shield Aura
Any of **your** pieces that is **defended by a friendly piece** glows with a pulsing gold shield. Pieces without a shield are alone — and easy targets.

### 🎯 Red Target Crosshair
A spinning red crosshair locks onto any **friendly piece that is attacked but undefended**. That's your "you're about to lose this!" alarm. Move the piece or defend it.

## ⏳ Time Spells — The Anti‑Blunder Magic

Every new game versus the AI gives you **3 Time Spells** (the glowing ⚡ crystals in the sidebar).

**How they work:**
1. You make a move that's a bad blunder (hangs a Queen, walks into check, etc.)
2. Merlin's **Temporal Foresight** modal pops up: *"Halt! I sense danger…"*
3. Click **✨ Cast Time Spell**:
   - The AI's punishing reply plays out for **2 seconds** so you can *see* what was about to happen
   - Then time rewinds to your move — you get to choose again
   - One spell crystal is consumed
4. Click **No, I am Brave!** to keep your move and face the music

Time Spells exist to teach: every blunder becomes a free lesson. Use them, learn from them, get stronger.

---

## ↩️ Takebacks

Don't like your last move? Click **↩️ Takeback** to undo it. Unlike Time Spells, this is unlimited — but it doesn't show you the consequence first.

## ⚔️ Choosing Your AI Difficulty

Pick your opponent in the sidebar:

| Level | Who they are | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| 🛡️ **Squire** | A novice opponent (Stockfish depth 1) | Total beginners |
| ⚔️ **Knight** | A solid amateur (depth 3) | Once you know the rules |
| 🏰 **Bishop** | A clever tactician (depth 6) | Confident players |
| 👑 **King** | A ruthless master (depth 12+) | Brave apprentices only |

You can change the level any time — even mid‑game from the **🆕 New Game** flow.

## 🪙 Earning Gold During a Game

While you play, you'll earn gold coins for:
- **Capturing** enemy pieces (bigger pieces = more gold)
- **Winning** the game
- **Solving puzzles** on the Quest Map
- **Unlocking badges** in the Hall of Fame

Gold buys cosmetics in the **Armoury Shop** (see Page 4).
